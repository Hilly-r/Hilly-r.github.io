PROJECT IDEA


My project idea is to create a website and reference for Australia’s national recycling needs. The site will use branding and design to represent recycling categories with a simple pictorial system inspired by the recycling symbol itself.

Using these symbols as the basis of the site, users will be able to search and quickly identify what is recyclable in their area. Information on category numbers will be succinct and backed up with images of examples along with newly designed corresponding icons. Another feature will be an interactive map, colour coded depending on how many recycling streams of the ten, are taken in given council areas identifying ‘green’ councils as those who take the most streams of waste and types of plastic, if not all. Searching for a council itself will display a simple cascade of icons, relevant to that councils recycling ability along with hot links to further information and a link to the council’s recycling page itself.



MOTIVATION

The motivation behind this is Australia’s large recycling problem. As a country we produce more waste and recycle less than the average of larger developed economies. Just half of our councils nation-wide accept all seven types of plastic, of which only 10 also accept plastic bags. This project is an attempt to garner more public awareness around a growing issue that is relatively well-acknowledged but less understood. Recycling is on the rise but so is the population and therefore waste amount. With the restrictions on exporting waste having come in to effect last year and promised funding by the government this website will be a useful tool to display needed, up-to-date, clear and concise instructions on what can and cannot be recycled in any given area within Australia.

There are two sites that exist – Planet arks “recycling near you” and Sustainability Victoria that are very informative, but they are heavily laden with information and users have to do more navigating around the websites than they should. Going to the councils themselves is a varied experience as each site displays their recycling information differently. Some giving lots of information and others very little. Creating a consistent display will unify the message of recycling and mean that every council displays the same relevant information in the one place.



TOOLS and TECHNOLOGY

The technology needed is not complex and already exists. Put simply, all that is required is readily available cross-platform software and a server to host the website.

Adobe InDesign and/or photoshop – for the creation of the recycling icons.
Microsoft office suite – for project documentation, presentations, statistics, budgeting and communicating.
Adobe Dreamweaver or similar– design the website itself.
A text editor (terminal, notepad, notepad++ etc.) – writing the code of and testing for the website.
All website browsers – (chrome, Firefox, safari, internet explorer) – for testing.
FileZilla – To transfer files onto the website server.
Slack/WhatsApp/messenger – For the team to communicate with one another and keep tabs on the job itself.
Coding languages: html5, css3, JavaScript, PHP.



SKILLS

The ability to use all the above software and write in the given code languages. Research, Graphic and website design, copywriting, communications, data collection and analysis.



OBSTACLES

The biggest obstacle will likely be having to cooperate with so many governing bodies and businesses. Getting full and correct details from each council and company and keeping it all up-to-date will mean the need for constant cooperation. The website will also be structurally complex due to the amount of data and information to be displayed so will have to be carefully mapped out in the designing phase, so that the simplicity of the site is not lost. The most essential part of this project is the simplicity and continuous design theme but with a topic that’s so important with so many facets it is easy to get side-tracked with the desire to display too much important information at once.



OUTCOMES

If successful, the website will be a nationwide tool that coherently displays relevant recycling information to everyone.

Other possibilities of broader scope can be planned for. Partnering with a mapping technology company (Google) could see every charity bin, op-shop, waste disposal centre, community garden (for compost) and clothing drop off shops (like H&M and ZARA) displayed within a given area and filtered for according to the users search requirements.

Linking pages can identify common recycling myths, profile waste disposal companies, show statistics and link to products made from recycled materials.

There are many other branches that can be explored but the main mission would always be to simplify recycling information through good design. Calling back to the icons so that they become synonymous with the materials they represent. The ultimate goal would be to increase public awareness and education to push pressure on to companies and governing bodies to have better recycling standards.
