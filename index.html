IT


My real interest in IT began after seeing the flexibility of IT jobs through a Software Developer friend. Really, I was jealous that he got to stay at home some days and could make the computer do things which I couldn’t. Something about the black screen filled with code reminded me of the fun DOS games I used to play in computer class and quite instantaneously after trying some basic coding myself, the world of technology and computing didn’t seem so ungraspable.

I think the most interesting thing about IT is its unmatchable reach and how quickly it has become a central structure in our daily lives. Our commute, workplace, homes and social lives have radically changed in every aspect due to IT. While technology has always made it’s advances and inevitable changes to the way we live, Modern IT has exponentially grown in a period less than three decades. I think this is because how accessible and simplified things become when everything is connected, and this interconnectedness is what most intrigues me. I also have a soft spot for cybersecurity and will never tire of hearing about STUXNET.

By studying IT, I hope to learn deep technical skills and the theory behind them so that I have a complete understanding of computing systems in general. Ultimately, I would like to learn the fundamentals of cyber security and how internet technologies integrate with one another. I would also like to learn how to better communicate my growing knowledge as a medium between technical experts and lay people.

IDEAL JOB

My Ideal IT job would be to become a Software Engineer or any of the job titles that fall under this banner.

The use of technical knowledge, requirement investigation, communicating with other people/stakeholders and design to solve complex problems is what most appeals about this role.

To become a software engineer you need an undergraduate degree in Computer Science or IT if not a straight software engineering degree. This needs to be followed by a number of years of experience as a software developer.


This job in particular would be amazing!

Wholefoods Software engineer

I love wholefoods food so this role – using C, C++, C#, Java or Perl to code for the technological implementation of large-scale business needs for a wholefoods/amazon service - would get me all-the-more closer to it.

For me to get this ideal job I’ll need to finish my studies in IT and advance quickly with my current coding skills. For work I will need to focus on more technical areas, but I believe being a business analyst with exposure to business workings and stakeholder management puts me in good stead to understand the business side of the job.
